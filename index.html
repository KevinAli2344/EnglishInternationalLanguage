• The English Language and Technology

Introduction

Technology has always been part of daily lives. Every day people use many technological devices that help them live more comfortably. Technology has influenced the social, economic, and political aspects of the world.

Language is also how new technologies become familiar with society. Every new innovation needs a name or an expression to be called.

English Language and Technological Inventions

Language contributes to technology in numerous ways including the creation of new words, new grammar rules, acronyms, and expressions. Acronyms are words created using only the initial letters of words that describe an object or a process. Examples include:

  • CD-ROM: Compact Disc Read Only Memory
  • USB: Universal Serial Bus
  • DVD: Digital Versatile Disc
  • LED: Light Emitting Diode
  • LASER: Light Amplification by Stimulated Emission of Radiation
  • GPS: Global Positioning System
  • HTTPS: Hypertext Transfer Protocol Secure
  • LCD: Liquid Crystal Display
  • RAM: Random Access Memory

Expressions like "going online" reflect the integration of technology into daily life.

The Early Years

In the Middle Ages, technology was closely related to military innovations. Examples include:

  • Arrow: Originated from Middle English "arwe," from Old English "Ä“arw-" and Proto-Germanic "*arhwo."
  • Sword: Derived from Old English and Proto-Indo-European "*swer-" meaning "to wound or cut."
  • Cannon: From 12th-century Old French "trebuchet," meaning "siege engine."

The Renaissance (1350-1650) marked a transitional period where inventions like the printing press by Johannes Gutenberg revolutionized communication.

The Industrial Revolution

The Industrial Revolution began in Great Britain in the late 1700s. Innovation led to large-scale machine manufacturing, transforming industries and societies.

The Arrival of the Internet

The Internet originated as ARPANET (Advanced Research Projects Agency Network) and became public on August 6, 1991. Initially met with skepticism, it transformed global communication and information sharing. "Netspeak" emerged as the language of the Internet, incorporating smileys and emoticons to express emotions.

Technology in English Learning

Since the 1960s, computers and technology have enhanced English learning through methods like:

  • Behavioristic CALL: Focused on repetitive exercises.
  • Communicative CALL: Emphasized implicit grammar teaching and original utterances.
  • Integrative CALL: Integrated multiple skills and advanced technologies.

Blended learning combines online materials and face-to-face methods, while tools like CALI's Ellis and DynEd's Dynamic English revolutionized teaching.

Virtual Learning Environments (VLEs)

VLEs are web-based platforms that enable teachers and students to organize work electronically. They facilitate resource sharing, discussions, and assessments. Innovations like Second Life offer immersive environments for language learning and interaction.
